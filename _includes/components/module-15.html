With a solid grip on his financial plan, and the recent sell of the old mini-van, Ryan decides for the first time ever in his life to purchase a brand-new car. After years of carefully planned second-hand purchasing, this car represents a special pleasure and proud accomplishment for Ryan. Dropping the kids, ages 11 and 9, at school suddenly takes on a new element of satisfaction.

They grow up so fast.

After what felt like a blink of his eye, Ryan’s children near completing their undergraduate studies. His daughter is at NTU and appears to be following her father’s footsteps. His own parents having passed away a few years earlier, Ryan now turns his mind to his own retirement.

In their final years, Ryan’s parents had no other option than to rely on Ryan for financial support. With his investments behaving as they should, and his fifties coming to a close, Ryan’s financial goals include preparation for his and Carrie’s final years, avoiding undue strain on their children for time and care.
